## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any analyst, clinician, or operator reading a stage-specific / segment-specific / cohort dashboard who infers "outcomes improved" from "every subgroup's average improved." The transferable correction: **"every subgroup improved" and "the population improved" are DIFFERENT claims that coincide only when group membership is FIXED.** When a classification boundary moves — finer diagnostics, a re-tiered segmentation, a re-bucketed cohort — members can be RELABELLED across the boundary, and if a reclassified member sits below its old group's mean and above its new group's mean, BOTH group means rise while no member's value and the population mean are unchanged (here LOW 0.25→0.30, HIGH 0.75→0.80, pooled fixed at 0.5, both subgroup means up at >=133σ). This is the Will Rogers phenomenon (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Rogers_phenomenon); its serious form is medical stage migration (Feinstein, Sosin & Wells, NEJM 1985), where finer staging inflates BOTH early- and late-stage survival with no change to any patient. To act on it: never compare subgroup means across a period in which the classification rule changed without checking the POPULATION mean and the migration counts; if the boundary moved, the subgroup improvements are (partly or wholly) a relabelling artifact, not a real gain. Two properties the verifier makes explicit: (i) the effect is EXACT conservation, not a behavioral magnitude — the pooled mean is invariant to the boundary by construction (a mathematical identity, cons_max_rel=0.0), so unlike a behavioral head there is no magnitude caveat, only the whole-vs-parts distinction; (ii) the direction matters — the disclosed model choice (HIGH→LOW, migrating the bottom slice of the higher group into the lower group it beats) is what makes BOTH means rise; a slice reaching above HIGH's mean would flip HIGH's sign.
