## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any retention / engagement lever whose designer assumes a "pressure" mechanism is monotone in its own strength — a rank-decay knob, a streak-loss penalty, an idle-timeout, any "use it or lose it" pressure meant to drive activity. The transferable correction: **a loss-framed pressure lever does NOT monotonically increase the behavior it targets — under realistic loss aversion (LAMBDA ≈ 2.25) the SIGN of its effect flips, because past a small optimum the marginal player QUITS (accumulated loss-averse pain crosses patience) instead of grinding back, so aggressive pressure CUTS total activity and CHURNS the population.** Here, at LAMBDA=2.25, aggressive rank decay cuts steady-state activity ~11.6% (657 → 581) while 291 of 1000 players churn, whereas the same decay under LAMBDA=1.0 is harmless (in fact slightly helpful).
