## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any game/economy designer who issues a second currency (a premium "hard" currency, a seasonal token, an event scrip) at nominal parity with an existing currency and expects it to circulate and "drive the economy." The transferable correction: **two currencies at nominal parity do NOT circulate equally when one is worth less to HOLD.** If either currency depreciates when hoarded (an inflating soft currency, a decaying resource, a token with an expiry or a wallet cap), rational players spend the depreciating (bad) one first and hoard the stable (good) one — so the good currency's circulation velocity collapses toward zero (here 0.69817 → 0.129105, an 81.5% collapse) and the bad currency does almost all the settlement, EXACTLY the reverse of the design intent (Gresham's law: bad money drives out good; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gresham%27s_law). Two caveats the verifier makes explicit: (i) the effect is MODEL-DEPENDENT — it requires rational (or at least loss-aware) hoarding; a purely naive proportional-spending population shows the OPPOSITE (good circulates more), so the sign hinges on how sophisticated the players are (the P024 discipline); (ii) the MAGNITUDE scales with the depreciation rate — a small melt buys a small gap, GAMMA→1 recovers the naive world. To act on it: if you WANT the premium currency to circulate, do not let it strictly dominate the soft currency as a store of value — narrow the depreciation gap, add a holding cost to the premium currency, or give it use-it-or-lose-it sinks; if you want a currency HOARDED (a prestige store of value), make it the stable one.
