## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any system that fronts an expensive origin (DB query, template render, upstream API, recompute) with a plain TTL cache and reasons about origin protection from the CACHE HIT RATIO. The transferable correction: **a near-100% hit ratio does NOT bound origin load — the worst-case per-expiry herd is Poisson(lambda·T) with expected size lambda·T, set entirely by the request rate and the recompute time, and it grows LINEARLY with traffic no matter how high the hit ratio climbs.** Caching a hot key concentrates its origin load into a burst at each expiry rather than eliminating it; a HOTTER key stampedes HARDER (the herd is 6× larger at 6× the rate). To act on it: **the cure for a stampede is request coalescing (single-flight / dogpile lock), NOT a higher hit ratio or a longer TTL** — coalescing collapses the per-expiry herd to exactly 1 recompute (1 − e^{−lambda·T}, ~1 for a hot key) independent of lambda, removing the ~lambda·T − 1 redundant recomputes a plain TTL cache never touches; lengthening the TTL only makes expiries rarer, it leaves the per-expiry herd size lambda·T untouched, so the worst-case burst is identical. Provisioning origin capacity for "average load ≈ misses × rate" under-provisions for the per-expiry burst by a factor of ~lambda·T. Anchors the cache-stampede / dogpile / thundering-herd literature (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cache_stampede); the herd = Poisson(lambda·T) and the coalesced collapse = 1 − e^{−lambda·T} are exact closed forms, checked here against the simulation at both rates (G1/G2).
