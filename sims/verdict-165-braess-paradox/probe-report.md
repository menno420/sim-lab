## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any network planner, traffic engineer, or capacity operator deciding whether to ADD a link (road, route, interconnect, queue-bypass) to a congested selfish-routing network. The transferable correction: **adding capacity to a network of self-interested users can make EVERYONE slower — a new zero-cost link is not "at worst unused"; at the selfish (Wardrop) equilibrium it can concentrate flow onto the very edges whose congestion it worsens, raising the equilibrium travel time for all above a demand threshold (here 30·CAP=3000).** To act on it: evaluate a proposed link at the SELFISH equilibrium the users will actually reach, not the social optimum a planner would choose, and check the demand regime — below the threshold the link helps, above it the link hurts, and the crossover is a calibrated function of capacity and fixed-edge latency. Deleting a road can speed traffic up (Braess 1968; Seoul Cheonggyecheon 2003, Stuttgart, NY 42nd Street). DISTINCT from a raw capacity intuition ("more roads = more throughput = less congestion"): here the added edge is strictly benevolent (option-only, can never lengthen a path) yet the equilibrium strictly worsens — the harm is an emergent property of non-cooperative routing, not of any single edge being slow.
