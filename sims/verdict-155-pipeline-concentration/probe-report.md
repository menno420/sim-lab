## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any attainment / threshold-crossing surface where a first-moment "coverage" or "health" ratio hides a second-moment concentration that sets the tail — a sales forecast, a capacity plan sized to expected demand, any `P(shortfall)` driven by the variance of a sum of size-weighted independent contributions. The transferable correction: **the pipeline coverage ratio (E[bookings]/quota) is NOT a sufficient statistic for quota attainment — at a fixed win-probability p and fixed total pipeline value V, Var(bookings)=p(1−p)·V²·HHI, so the variance ratio between two same-coverage pipelines equals their deal-value HHI ratio exactly, and a whale-concentrated book misses quota far more often than a granular book at the SAME coverage.** For bookings B=Σ Xᵢvᵢ over iid Bernoulli(p) win indicators, HHI=Σwᵢ² (wᵢ=vᵢ/V) is the Herfindahl concentration of deal-value shares: at 3× coverage the 8-whale book (HHI≈0.235) misses ~15.6% while the 75-deal granular book (HHI≈0.013) essentially never misses. To act on it: never treat "3× coverage" as attainment insurance — report the deal-value HHI (or the effective deal count 1/HHI) alongside the coverage ratio, and treat a whale-concentrated book at a given coverage as materially riskier than a granular book at the same coverage; coverage fixes the mean, concentration sets the miss tail.
