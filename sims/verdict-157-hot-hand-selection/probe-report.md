## Transferable correction (lane consumer, Q-0264)
Lane CONSUMER = any analysis that estimates "what happens right after event X?" by conditioning on a run of X in FINITE data and averaging a per-window RATIO — a "hit rate after a hot streak", a "conversion after N good touches", a "failure rate after a run of successes", any post-conditioning-window fraction computed within finite sequences and averaged across them. The transferable correction: **a post-streak success fraction below the base rate on a MEMORYLESS fair process is a finite-sample SELECTION artifact of the ratio estimator (Miller & Sanjurjo, Econometrica 2018), NOT evidence of negative autocorrelation or "mean reversion".** Conditioning on "the previous K outcomes were all successes" and averaging the within-sequence fraction of the immediately-following outcomes induces a negative dependence between selecting a flip and that flip being a success, so the expected fraction is strictly below the true rate (here E[fraction]=0.35289 vs the true 0.5 on a fair coin) — and the shortfall is real, exactly enumerable, and vanishes only as sequence length → ∞. To act on it: **a base-rate-below post-streak read does NOT disprove a hot hand (or prove "streaks reverse")** — the correct null is the exact finite-sample expectation b(N,K) computed by enumeration (0.35289 here), not the unconditional base rate (0.5); comparing an observed post-streak rate to 0.5 instead of to b(N,K) will manufacture a spurious "anti-streak" (or mask a genuine one). This is what re-opened the hot-hand debate: the original "fallacy" studies compared post-streak shooting to the base rate and read the built-in selection bias as evidence AGAINST streakiness.
