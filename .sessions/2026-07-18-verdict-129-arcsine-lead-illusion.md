## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the arcsine law on the FIRST arcsine statistic — the OCCUPATION TIME (fraction of steps one side spends in the lead) — and proves it is U-shaped, not concentrated at 1/2. But Lévy's arcsine family has THREE members that share the exact same arcsine CDF (2/π)arcsin√x yet answer different operational questions, and the current gate exercises only the first. The SECOND arcsine law governs the LAST-ZERO / last-tie time: the last moment the fair contest was level is also arcsine-distributed, so a fair match is most likely to have had its final lead-change either very early or very late — never comfortably in the middle — which is the load-bearing statistic for STOPPING RULES (when is a lead "safe"?) and for reading a scoreboard mid-game. The THIRD (the arcsine law of the MAXIMUM / argmax) governs WHEN the peak lead occurs, arcsine-distributed over the game's duration, which is the statistic a trailing competitor cares about ("has the leader already peaked?"). A genuinely different next UNRELATED slice pins the SECOND arcsine law as a distinct, seed-pinnable mechanism: on the SAME fair ±1 walk of 2n steps, measure the index of the LAST return to zero L (the last tie), and pre-register (G-a) a last-tie-is-U-shaped gate — the measured P(L/2n ≤ LEAD_LO or ≥ LEAD_HI) ≥ EXT_MIN by ≥3σ, and its lower-tail matches the exact discrete last-visit law P(last zero at 2k)=u_{2k}·u_{2(n−k)} (the SAME product form as occupation time — a beautiful equidistribution) within 3σ; (G-b) a middle-is-rare control — P(L/2n in [BAL_LO,BAL_HI]) ≤ BAL_MAX by ≥3σ; and (G-c) an equidistribution discriminator — the last-tie fraction and the lead-time fraction share a CDF (their empirical distributions agree by a two-sample KS/quantile gate within 3σ) YET are DIFFERENT random variables on each path (their per-path correlation is < 1 by ≥3σ), so the operator lesson sharpens from "a fair game's lead is U-shaped" to "the fraction-of-time-led, the last-tie moment, and the peak-lead moment are all arcsine-distributed and hence all give the SAME misleading 'someone dominated' signal in a perfectly fair contest — so budget stopping rules and 'is the lead safe' judgments to the arcsine tails of the LAST-TIE law, not to occupation time alone."
