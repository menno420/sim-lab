## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the ALL-of-N (wait-for-the-last) fan-out with a SINGLE homogeneous per-leaf slow probability p and INDEPENDENT leaves — the pure union bound P_slow(N)=1−(1−p)^N. A genuinely different NEXT fleet slice, distinct from this all-of-N head and from the hedged-request tail cure (P121/V134, which prices the p95-deferred second copy), pins the **quorum / k-of-N tail collapse**: a scatter-gather that returns as soon as the FASTEST N−k+1 of N leaves respond (tolerating up to k−1 stragglers — partial results, best-of-R replica reads, a batch that needs only a quorum) is slow only when MORE than k−1 leaves are slow, so the request-slow probability drops from the union 1−(1−p)^N to the binomial upper tail P(Binom(N,p) ≥ k), and the median crossover pushes far past N*=0.693/p. The pre-registrable structure: (G-a) at k=1 (tolerate zero stragglers = wait-for-all) the quorum tail reproduces this slice's exact union 1−(1−p)^N (the k=1 boundary IS the all-of-N case); (G-b) for a fixed straggler budget k the MC request-slow fraction tracks the exact binomial upper tail P(Binom(N,p) ≥ k) within 3σ, decisively below the union bound — one tolerated straggler (k=2) at N=100, p=0.01 drops the slow rate from 63.4% to ~26.4% (1 − 0.99^100 − 100·0.01·0.99^99); (G-c) a DOSE-RESPONSE control shows the slow rate is strictly decreasing in the straggler budget k and the median crossover N*_k grows super-linearly in k, with a ≥3σ separation between k=1 and k=2 at fixed N — isolating quorum depth, not fan-out width, as the tail lever. The operator lesson sharpens from "waiting for all N leaves inherits a 1−(1−p)^N tail that bites at ~69 leaves" to "tolerate a small straggler budget (return on a quorum, not the last leaf) — one tolerated straggler converts the union tail into a binomial upper tail and buys back most of the amplification for a bounded correctness cost." A seed-pinnable reliability mechanism deliberately distinct from this all-of-N union head and the hedged-request cure.
