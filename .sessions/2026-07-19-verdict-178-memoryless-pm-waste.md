# VERDICT 178 — memoryless-pm-waste: preventive maintenance on a memoryless / constant-hazard (exponential) component is pure waste — because the exponential is MEMORYLESS the residual life of a used part has the SAME distribution as a new part, so scheduled preventive replacement does NOT lower the failure rate, it only adds replacement cost; the failure rate under a preventive-replacement policy EQUALS the run-to-failure rate λ regardless of the replacement interval τ, so PM buys strictly higher total maintenance cost for zero reliability gain, and the benefit only appears once the hazard is INCREASING (Weibull shape k > 1, wear-out)

Reproduce PROPOSAL 165 (P165 → V178, +13): memoryless PM waste — in a RELIABILITY / maintenance model a component with an exponential lifetime has a CONSTANT hazard rate λ, so by the memoryless property P(T > s + t | T > s) = P(T > t) the residual life of a part that has survived to age s is distributed identically to a brand-new part. Under the pinned world a preventive-replacement (age-replacement) policy that swaps the part at a fixed interval τ therefore leaves the long-run failure rate UNCHANGED at λ — the interval τ is invisible to the failure process — while it strictly ADDS the cost of the preventive swaps, so on a constant-hazard component preventive maintenance is pure waste. The folk belief (inverted here): "preventive maintenance always helps — replacing a part before it fails on a fixed schedule reduces the failure rate and extends effective life, so a shorter PM interval always buys fewer failures." It is NOT: on a MEMORYLESS (exponential, constant-hazard) component a used part is as-good-as-new, so the failure rate under an age-replacement policy is exactly λ for EVERY interval τ — preventive replacement changes no failure statistic and only burns replacement cost; the reliability benefit of PM is a property of an INCREASING hazard (wear-out / IFR), and it VANISHES the instant the hazard is constant.
