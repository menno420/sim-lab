# VERDICT 155 — pipeline coverage concentration risk

Reproduce PROPOSAL 142 (round-33 VENTURE slot, P142 → V155, +13): the sales "pipeline coverage ratio" (expected pipeline value ÷ quota) is NOT a sufficient statistic for quota attainment. For bookings B = Σ Xᵢ·vᵢ with iid Bernoulli(p) win indicators Xᵢ over deals of value vᵢ (total V), E[B] = p·V and Var(B) = p(1−p)·Σvᵢ² = p(1−p)·V²·HHI, where HHI = Σwᵢ² is the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration of deal-value shares wᵢ = vᵢ/V. Folk belief (inverted here): "get to 3× coverage and attainment takes care of itself — coverage is a sufficient statistic for quota attainment." FALSE. Coverage E[B]/quota fixes ONLY the first moment (p and V), so two pipelines with the SAME p and SAME V have identical coverage and identical expected bookings; yet at fixed p and V the variance RATIO equals the HHI RATIO exactly (p(1−p) and V² cancel), so a book concentrated in a few whales (high HHI) has far more bookings variance and misses quota far more often than a granular book (HHI → 1/N floor) at the SAME coverage. Mechanism: coverage is blind to concentration — the miss-probability tail P(B<quota) is set by Var(B) ∼ HHI, not by the coverage ratio.
