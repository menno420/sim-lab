## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the Young–Daly optimum on a MEMORYLESS failure process — Poisson(1/M) crashes, exponential inter-failure, constant hazard — which is exactly what makes the optimal interval a single CONSTANT τ*=√(2CM) independent of how long the current segment has already survived. But real long-running-job failures are frequently NOT memoryless: aging / resource-leak / thermal wear-out give an INCREASING hazard (Weibull shape k>1 — the longer a segment runs the likelier the next instant crashes), and infant-mortality / cold-start flakiness gives a DECREASING hazard (k<1). Under a non-exponential hazard the Young–Daly derivation's load-bearing step (expected recompute ≈ τ/2, "a crash lands on average halfway through the interval") FAILS — an increasing hazard concentrates crashes near the END of a long segment, so the expected lost work is more than τ/2 and the constant √(2CM) interval systematically UNDER-checkpoints an aging job. The transferable generalization the current gate does NOT exercise is that the optimal checkpoint policy under a non-constant hazard is no longer a constant interval but an AGE-DEPENDENT schedule: checkpoint MORE often as the segment ages under wear-out (k>1), LESS often under infant-mortality (k<1), and the constant Young–Daly τ* is recovered ONLY in the memoryless special case k=1. A genuinely different next FLEET slice pins the non-memoryless-checkpoint mechanism: drive the SAME W-work checkpoint/restart job but with Weibull(k,λ) inter-failure draws instead of `expovariate`, and pre-register (G-a) a MIS-SIZING gate — under Weibull k>1 the fixed Young–Daly τ*=√(2CM) wastes strictly more total wall time than the age-optimal schedule by ≥3σ (the memoryless formula mis-sizes a non-memoryless job); (G-b) a MONOTONICITY gate — the age-optimal inter-checkpoint gap is monotone DECREASING in elapsed segment-age under k>1 by ≥3σ (an aging job earns a shrinking safe interval); and (G-c) a REDUCTION gate — at k=1 the age-optimal schedule collapses back to the constant Young–Daly τ* within 3σ (the exponential special case is recovered) — converting this slice's single-number "set τ*=√(2CM)" into the actionable "the constant Young–Daly interval is the memoryless special case; under aging hazards the checkpoint interval must track the job's age." A seed-pinnable Weibull-hazard checkpoint mechanism distinct from this slice's constant-hazard head and from the outbox's single-optimum frame.
