# VERDICT 139 — partner-channel margin stacking / double marginalization

Reproduce PROPOSAL 126 (round-29 VENTURE slot, P126 → V139, +13): partner-channel margin stacking — the go-to-market / channel-economics trap. The folk model of adding a channel partner (distributor, reseller, VAR, marketplace, agency that buys wholesale and re-sells at its own price) is subtractive: "the partner takes a cut, so we keep list price minus their margin" — the partner's margin read as a transfer, a slice of a fixed pie handed over in exchange for reach. It is not a transfer. When TWO firms in series each independently set a profit-maximizing markup — the manufacturer picks a wholesale price w over its cost C, then the reseller picks a retail price P over the wholesale price it now faces as ITS cost — the two markups STACK (double marginalization, Cournot 1838 / Spengler 1950). Each firm marks up ignoring the margin the other already takes, so the street price lands ABOVE what a single vertically-integrated seller (one markup, cost→monopoly price) would charge, and the manufacturer's profit PLUS the reseller's profit is strictly LESS than the one integrated firm's — a deadweight loss, not a redistribution (the integrated price already maximizes total channel profit, so any second independent markup can only lower it). Two facts the "the partner just takes a cut" instinct misses: (1) the independent channel RAISES the street price above the direct-sale monopoly price (two hands on the pricing lever overshoot the joint optimum), not below it; (2) it SHRINKS the total pie — vendor + partner COMBINED earn less than one integrated firm, at a higher price and lower quantity, so consumers pay more and buy less. On linear demand (WTP ~ Uniform[0,VMAX], VMAX≈100, C=20) the two-stage FOCs give P_int=(VMAX+C)/2=60, w*=(VMAX+C)/2=60, P_dec=(3·VMAX+C)/4=80, so the decentralized channel price exceeds the integrated by (VMAX−C)/4=20 and the combined channel profit is exactly 3/4 of integrated at exactly 1/2 the quantity — the classic double-marginalization ratios. The cure is to remove the second independent markup: a two-part tariff (wholesale AT COST + a fixed franchise fee), a resale-price ceiling / RPM, a revenue-share that makes the partner price as if it owned the full margin, or vertical integration / agency pricing.
