Reproduce PROPOSAL 144 (round-33 UNRELATED slot, P144 → V157, +13): the Miller–Sanjurjo hot-hand selection bias. In a FINITE sequence of N=15 fair, memoryless coin flips, select every flip that immediately follows a run of K=3 consecutive heads and measure the fraction of those selected flips that are heads; the EXPECTED value of that per-sequence fraction is strictly BELOW 0.5 — not 0.5 — even though the coin is fair. The counterintuitive part: a memoryless fair process, measured with the natural "what happens right after a streak?" estimator, looks anti-streaky. This is a finite-sample SELECTION/sampling artifact — conditioning on "the previous K flips were heads" and averaging the RATIO across finite sequences induces a within-sequence negative dependence between selecting a flip and that flip being heads — NOT any real negative autocorrelation in the coin. The verifier computes the EXACT finite-sequence expectation by exhaustive enumeration of all 2^N=32768 sequences (closed-form reference; sequences with no post-streak flip are undefined and dropped) and an independent Monte-Carlo estimate over M=400000 sequences, then evaluates three ordered gates: G1 estimator-agreement (two independent halves of the M per-sequence fractions do NOT significantly disagree, |z|<3) → G2 closed-form agreement (the MC mean is NOT significantly different from the exact enumerated expectation, |z|<3) → G3 head (the MC mean is SIGNIFICANTLY below 0.5, z=(0.5−mean)/se ≥ 3). On the pinned world (SEED=20260717, N_FLIPS=15, K_STREAK=3, M_SEQUENCES=400000, SIGMA_GATE=3.0) all three gates hold in order and the run is deterministic. The trap: a practitioner who observes a below-0.5 post-streak head rate on fair data and concludes "streaks reverse / no hot hand" is reading a finite-sample selection artifact, not evidence against streakiness (the P024 model-dependence discipline applied to an estimator, not a model).

## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the selection bias at ONE sequence length (N=15), ONE streak length (K=3), and contrasts the post-streak head fraction against exactly ONE reference point (0.5). A genuinely distinct NEXT unrelated-slot slice, distinct from this single-point fixed-(N,K) head, pins the **magnitude of the Miller–Sanjurjo bias as a function of the (N, K) pair**: because the bias arises from finite-sequence conditioning, the expected post-streak head fraction is a closed-form function b(N, K) = E[fraction] that (i) is strictly below 0.5 for all finite N, (ii) grows in magnitude (further below 0.5) as K rises at fixed N (a longer required streak leaves fewer, more selection-distorted post-streak flips), and (iii) shrinks toward 0.5 as N → ∞ at fixed K (the finite-sample artifact vanishes in the limit). The pre-registrable structure: (G-a) confirm b(N, K) < 0.5 by ≥3σ across an (N, K) grid via exact enumeration (the bias is universal in finite N, not an N=15 accident); (G-b) a monotonicity headline — confirm |bias| is strictly INCREASING in K at fixed N by ≥3σ (longer streaks bias harder); (G-c) a vanishing-limit control — confirm |bias| strictly DECREASES as N grows at fixed K by ≥3σ, converging toward 0 (the artifact is finite-sample, so a large-N base-rate read is nearly unbiased). The operator lesson sharpens from "a below-0.5 post-streak fraction on fair data is a selection artifact" to "the artifact's SIZE is set by how long a streak you condition on relative to your sequence length — short sequences and long required streaks bias hardest, and the correction is the exact enumerated b(N,K), not 0.5." A seed-pinnable pure-combinatorics mechanism deliberately distinct from this single-(N,K) head, and it would convert the current single-point head into a first-class question (how does the bias scale?) with an exact closed-form reference at every grid point.
