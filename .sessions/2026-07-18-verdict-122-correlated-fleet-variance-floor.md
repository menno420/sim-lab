## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins a SINGLE homogeneous equicorrelation — every pair of agents shares the SAME rho=0.25 — and gates the flat floor rho*sigma^2 with N_eff→1/rho. But real fleets are not equicorrelated: correlation CLUSTERS (agents sharing an upstream/model/prior have high within-cluster correlation, agents across independent upstreams have low across-cluster correlation). A genuinely different next FLEET slice pins a BLOCK-equicorrelation structure and shows the floor is governed by the number of INDEPENDENT clusters, not the fleet size: partition N agents into K clusters of size m=N/K, error eps_i = sqrt(rho_b)·G + sqrt(rho_w−rho_b)·H_{c(i)} + sqrt(1−rho_w)·Z_i (a global common-mode G, a per-cluster common-mode H shared only within cluster c(i), and idiosyncratic Z_i), so within-cluster correlation is rho_w and across-cluster is rho_b<rho_w. Pre-register: (G-a) the fleet-mean MSE floors at the exact block formula rho_b·sigma^2 + (rho_w−rho_b)·sigma^2/K + (1−rho_w)·sigma^2/N within 3σ — so as N→∞ at fixed K the MSE floors at rho_b·sigma^2 + (rho_w−rho_b)·sigma^2/K, and N_eff→1/rho_b is only reached by raising the CLUSTER COUNT K, not the headcount; (G-b) a collapse control where rho_b=rho_w reproduces THIS slice's flat equicorrelation floor (rho*sigma^2+(1−rho)sigma^2/N) within 3σ, tying the block model back to the measured baseline; and (G-c) a diversification-lever gate — at fixed total N, doubling K (halving cluster size) cuts the MSE toward the rho_b floor by a pre-registered, closed-form-anchored amount, while doubling N at fixed K does essentially nothing (bounded below the G3 noise floor). That converts this slice's flat "adding agents past N~1/rho buys nothing" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "N_eff is set by the across-cluster correlation and the number of independent upstreams K — diversify the common-mode (more independent clusters), do not scale headcount within a cluster" — a seed-pinnable hierarchical-correlation mechanism distinct from this slice's flat-equicorrelation floor and from the outbox's flat 1/rho assertion.
