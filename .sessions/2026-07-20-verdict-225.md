## What this verdict does

Reproduces PROPOSAL 212 (P212 → V225, +13 offset, lane UNRELATED — closes the round-50
fleet→venture→game→unrelated cycle): **adding a third axis flips certain return into probable never-return.**
A simple symmetric nearest-neighbour random walk on the integer lattice returns to its starting point with
probability 1 in dimensions 1 and 2 (RECURRENT), but with probability only ~0.3405 in dimension 3
(TRANSIENT) — it escapes to infinity and never comes back about 66% of the time. Pólya proved this in 1921;
Kakutani's aphorism captures it: "A drunk man will find his way home, but a drunk bird may be lost forever."
The surprise is entirely in one integer step of the dimension: the return-probability summand p_d(2n) decays
like n^(−d/2), so the Green's-function series ΣU diverges for d≤2 (recurrent, return prob 1) and converges
for d≥3 (transient, return prob 1−1/U). Copies the disclosed verifier
