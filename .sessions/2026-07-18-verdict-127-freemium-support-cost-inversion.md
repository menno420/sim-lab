## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the freemium inversion on a DETERMINISTIC intent DECAY — p(i)=P0·e^{−K·i} is a smooth monotone curve, so the contribution optimum n*=ln(V·P0/c)/K is a single clean closed-form point and "meter against n*, not signups" is exact. But real acquisition intent is almost never a clean exponential in cumulative volume: channels are LUMPY (a high-intent SEO cohort, then a low-intent paid-social blast, then a mid-intent referral wave), so realized p(i) is a piecewise/mixture curve whose marginal contribution can cross zero, go back POSITIVE on a later high-intent channel, and cross again — the concave-single-peak assumption breaks and the naive "stop at the first zero-crossing" rule leaves value on the table (or over-cuts a channel that would have recovered). The load-bearing generalization the current gate does NOT exercise is CHANNEL-MIX contribution accounting: for a free-tier fed by K channels each with its own intent distribution and its own per-user cost c_k, the contribution-maximizing policy is not a single volume cap n* but a per-channel admit rule (admit channel k's marginal cohort iff its expected conversion clears c_k/V) — the SAME breakeven identity applied per channel, which can admit a cheap high-intent channel to far past the blended n* while cutting an expensive low-intent one entirely. A genuinely different next VENTURE slice pins the heterogeneous-channel free-tier mechanism: hold the marginal(i)=V·p−c frame but draw a MIXTURE intent process (K channels, each an intent level × a cost c_k × a volume) and pre-register (G-a) a per-channel breakeven gate — the optimal admit boundary for each channel matches its closed-form p_k ≥ c_k/V within 3σ (so an operator reads "admit this channel while its conversion clears its own serving cost", not a single blended cap); (G-b) a mix-inversion gate — a blended single-n* cap leaves a measured ≥3σ contribution gap vs the per-channel policy when channels are cost-heterogeneous (proving the single-cap rule is strictly dominated once cost varies by channel); and (G-c) a non-concavity control — with a low-intent channel ordered BEFORE a high-intent one, total contribution is NON-monotone-to-peak (a second positive-marginal region after a zero-crossing), gated by detecting the second crossing at ≥3σ — converting this slice's clean single-peak "cap at n*" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "meter each channel against its OWN c_k/V breakeven, because with mixed channels the blended optimum both mis-caps and can hide a recoverable high-intent cohort past the first zero-crossing" — a seed-pinnable heterogeneous-channel-economics mechanism distinct from this slice's homogeneous exponential decay and from the outbox's single-curve concave-contribution frame.
