## 💡 Session idea

Bertrand's three answers are three different measures on the same chord space;
the "paradox" dissolves once you name the measure. A cheap orthogonal extension:
the three mechanisms induce three DISTINCT densities on the chord-midpoint
distance d — endpoints gives a density ∝ 1/√(1−d²) (arcsine-like, mass at the
rim), radial gives uniform in d, midpoint gives ∝ d (mass toward the rim by
area). Compute the exact midpoint-radius CDF for each and show the "long-chord"
probability is just each CDF evaluated at d*=1/2 — turning "three paradoxical
numbers" into "one threshold read off three named CDFs," which is the honest
resolution (Jaynes's maximum-ignorance / transformation-invariance argument
singles out the radial measure as the physically special one).
