# VERDICT 160 — Gresham currency hoarding: good money is hoarded out of circulation, bad money circulates

Reproduce PROPOSAL 147 (round-34 GAME slot, P147 → V160, +13): Gresham currency hoarding. A game economy issues TWO currencies at nominal parity — a STABLE "good" currency (holds its value) and a DEPRECIATING "bad" currency (a holding penalty melts a fraction 1−GAMMA of any UNSPENT bad balance each period). Both faucets mint SYMMETRIC nominal income (each period every player draws good units and bad units from the SAME Uniform[0,INC_MAX]) and both settle a per-period real expense E~Uniform[0,E_MAX] at par (1 unit of either = 1 real). The folk belief: "issue a premium hard currency at par with the soft currency and it will circulate and drive the economy — two interchangeable currencies at parity spend equally." Reality (Gresham's law, bad money drives out good): RATIONAL players settle the melting bad currency FIRST (from_bad=min(pay,bad_wallet), from_good=pay−from_bad) and HOARD the stable good one, so the good currency's circulation velocity COLLAPSES toward zero and the bad currency does almost all the settlement — even though the faucets are symmetric. Good money is driven OUT of circulation, exactly because it is good. The SIGN of the circulation gap (share of real settlement done in bad currency minus share done in good) depends on spender RATIONALITY: NAIVE proportional payers (pay each expense in proportion to current unit holdings, no currency preference) actually settle MORE in good — because the good currency never melts it accumulates in wallets — so the naive gap is NEGATIVE (good circulates more, no Gresham effect); RATIONAL hoarders make the gap strongly POSITIVE (good hoarded out). A model-dependence result (the P024 discipline): with no rational hoarding the folk belief holds; with rational hoarding the lever flips sign and the good currency's velocity collapses. The verifier runs N=1000 players × T=100 periods × R=40 paired trials under common random numbers (the naive and rational worlds share the per-trial seed SEED + r·TRIAL_STRIDE) and evaluates three ordered gates on the /se convention (z=mean/se, SIGMA_GATE=3.0): G1 AGREEMENT (two independent halves of the R rational-world circulation gaps agree within noise, |z|<3) → G2 CONTROL (the naive proportional world's circulation gap is NOT significantly positive, z<3 — no Gresham effect without hoarding) → G3 HEAD (the rational-hoarder world's circulation gap is SIGNIFICANTLY positive, z≥3 — good driven out of circulation). On the pinned world (SEED=20260717) all three gates hold in order and the run is deterministic. The trap: a designer who issues a premium hard currency at par expecting it to circulate and drive the economy is ignoring that rational players hoard the currency that holds value and spend the one that melts — so the "good" currency's velocity collapses toward zero.
