# VERDICT 135 — NRR composition mirage

Reproduce PROPOSAL 122 (round-28 VENTURE slot, P122 → V135, +13): the NRR composition mirage — dollar-weighting hides logo churn. Net Revenue Retention (net dollar retention) is the headline SaaS health metric, and it is DOLLAR-weighted: NRR=Σr_i·m_i/Σr_i = Σw_i·m_i, a revenue-share-weighted mean (w_i=r_i/Σr) of each account's retained-revenue multiple m_i (0 if churned, the expansion factor if it survived and grew). When revenue is concentrated in a few large accounts that expand while a long tail of small accounts churns, the whales' dollars DOMINATE the numerator and the blended NRR reads ABOVE 100% ("negative net churn", best-in-class) even though the MAJORITY OF LOGOS were lost — Simpson's paradox in a weighted mean. Two facts the "NRR>100% = we retain customers" instinct misses: (1) NRR and logo (count-weighted) retention DIVERGE — a dollar-NRR of 118% coexists with 63.5% logo retention (a third of customers gone), so "negative net churn" is silent on how many customers stayed; (2) the >100% is the WEIGHTING, not the retention — strip the revenue weights (equal-weight every account, NRR_equal=mean_i m_i) and the identical book reads BELOW 100%, contracting per logo. The headline was manufactured by concentration and flips sign the moment every account counts equally. So NRR is a revenue-CONCENTRATION statistic, not a customer-retention one.
