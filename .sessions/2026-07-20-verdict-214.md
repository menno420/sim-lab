## What this verdict does

Reproduces PROPOSAL 201 (P201 → V214, +13 offset): Graham's multiprocessing timing
anomaly. Greedy non-preemptive LIST SCHEDULING of precedence-constrained jobs on `m`
identical machines is NON-monotone — *relaxing* an input can make the makespan *worse*.
Three independent relaxations each demonstrate it: adding a machine (`m → m+1`),
shortening every job by 1, or deleting a precedence edge can each STRICTLY INCREASE the
makespan. Yet the greedy makespan is provably bounded `list ≤ (2 − 1/m)·OPT`
(Graham 1969). Copies the disclosed verifier `ideas/fleet/graham_scheduling_anomaly.py`
byte-identical into `sims/verdict-214-graham-anomaly/`, reproduces the results-dict
sha256, confirms determinism, and evaluates the gates in order against the proposal's
OWN criteria.
