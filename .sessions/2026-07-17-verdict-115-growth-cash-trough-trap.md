## 💡 Session idea
The trap is proven on a KNOWN, CONSTANT growth rate g held fixed for all T periods, but the operational founder failure mode is that g is a DECISION the operator revises as cash gets tight — and the natural reaction to a deepening trough is to CUT acquisition, which itself has delayed cash effects (the CAC you stop paying saves cash immediately, but the margin from customers you didn't acquire is lost over their whole lifetime). A genuinely different next VENTURE slice pins a CLOSED-LOOP g_t controller: let the operator throttle g_t as a function of the current cash position C_t (e.g. cut growth when C_t dips below a fraction of K0), and pre-register two gates — (1) a NAIVE reactive controller that cuts g the period AFTER the trough breaches a threshold still ruins at ≥3σ more often than a controller that anticipates the g_crit boundary, because the churn/margin lag means the cash bleed continues for periods after g is cut (the trough OVERSHOOTS the trigger), and (2) the overshoot magnitude scales monotonically with the churn survival s (slower churn ⇒ longer margin lag ⇒ deeper overshoot after the cut). That converts the static "stay below g_crit" rule into the actionable "cut growth BEFORE the trough, not after — the cash bleed has a lifetime-long tail," a seed-pinnable, ≥3σ-gateable mechanism (control lag on an accrual system) distinct from this slice's open-loop constant-g order-of-magnitude result.
