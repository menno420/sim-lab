## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins a SINGLE-SKU, SINGLE-DEPTH promotion (one product at margin m=0.40, one depth d=0.20) and gates the flat breakeven uplift u*(d)=d/(m−d) against a homogeneous exponential-WTP population. But real promotional planning is a PORTFOLIO problem across a catalog of SKUs with heterogeneous margins and elasticities under a fixed markdown budget — and the convexity that makes u*(d) explode near d=m is exactly what a portfolio optimizer must exploit, not just avoid. A genuinely different next VENTURE slice pins a MULTI-SKU markdown-allocation mechanism and shows the profit-maximizing depth vector is governed by an elasticity-ranked water-filling rule, not by uniform discounting: K SKUs each with its own margin m_k and measured elasticity λ_k P0, a total promotional-depth (or expected-units-sold) budget B, and per-SKU gross-profit change ΔGP_k(d_k)=GP_k(0)·[e^{λ_k P0 d_k}·(m_k−d_k)/m_k − 1]. Pre-register: (G-a) the constrained-optimal depth allocation puts ALL budget on SKUs whose elasticity clears their own breakeven λ*_k=ln(m_k/(m_k−d_k))/(P0 d_k) and ZERO on those below it — a bang-bang / knapsack structure the naive "discount everything 20%" strategy violates, gateable by ≥3σ profit gap between the optimal and uniform allocations; (G-b) a Lagrangian water-filling control where the marginal ΔGP per unit of budget is equalized across all funded SKUs at the optimum (KKT stationarity), reproduced within 3σ against the closed-form multiplier; and (G-c) a convexity-trap gate — as any SKU's optimal depth approaches its margin m_k, its marginal profit contribution turns sharply negative (the d→m asymptote of THIS slice), so the optimizer caps depth strictly below m_k by a pre-registered, closed-form-anchored margin. That converts this slice's single-SKU "size the depth against d/(m−d) for the measured elasticity" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "allocate a fixed markdown budget by elasticity-ranked water-filling, fund only SKUs past their own λ*, and cap every depth below its margin" — a seed-pinnable portfolio-markdown mechanism distinct from this slice's single-SKU breakeven and from the outbox's per-SKU λ* assertion.
