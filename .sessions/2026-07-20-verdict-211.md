## What this verdict does

Reproduces PROPOSAL 198 (P198 → V211, +13 offset): Weitzman's Pandora's Box
reservation-index rule. In sequential search with costly inspection (Weitzman
1979), N closed boxes each cost `c_i` to open and reveal a reward `R_i` from a
known discrete distribution; you may stop any time and keep the single best reward
(outside option 0, no discounting). The optimal policy does NOT open boxes in
decreasing order of expected reward — it assigns each box a RESERVATION VALUE `z_i`
solving

        c_i = E[(R_i − z_i)^+]

opens boxes in decreasing `z_i`, and stops the moment the best reward in hand ≥ the
largest reservation value among still-closed boxes. Exactly-true consequences: you
can rationally decline a box whose mean beats your best; a lower-mean, higher-spread
box can be optimal to open FIRST; and the index-rule value EXACTLY equals the
brute-force optimum over ALL policies. Copies the disclosed verifier
