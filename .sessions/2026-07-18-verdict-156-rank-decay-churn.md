# VERDICT 156 — rank-decay churn cliff

Reproduce PROPOSAL 143 (round-33 GAME slot, P143 → V156, +13): on a competitive ranked ladder, RANK DECAY (an idle player loses rank over time) does NOT monotonically raise activity — under loss aversion the folk lever INVERTS. Each of N players has a fixed true_rank and a moving current_rank (both start at 0 → underrank = −current_rank); per-match win probability p = clip(0.5 − K·current_rank, 0.05, 0.95) (underranked players win more — the folk "grind back" recovery channel). Each period a player draws a FRESH per-match enjoyment u ~ Uniform[0, U_MAX] and forms the loss-averse subjective match value v = u + DELTA·(p − LAMBDA·(1 − p)): a prospective rank GAIN is valued +DELTA, a rank LOSS −LAMBDA·DELTA (loss aversion, LAMBDA ≥ 1). If v > 0 the player PLAYS (win w.p. p: current_rank += DELTA, else −= DELTA), counts one unit of activity, and resets idle pain to 0; otherwise the player is idle — apply decay current_rank −= d (raising future underrank and hence future p, the folk recovery channel), accumulate pain += LAMBDA·d, and if pain > PATIENCE the player QUITS permanently (churn). Folk belief (inverted here): "rank decay keeps a ladder healthy; more decay = more activity." FALSE under realistic loss aversion — aggressive decay pushes marginal players to QUIT (pain crosses PATIENCE before the recovery channel pulls them back), so the SIGN of decay's effect on activity is set by LAMBDA: with no loss aversion (LAMBDA=1) decay is harmless, with realistic loss aversion (LAMBDA=2.25, the Kahneman–Tversky prospect-theory coefficient) decay backfires. On the pinned world (SEED=20260717, per-trial seed SEED + r·7919 with common random numbers across the decay-0 baseline and decay-D_HIGH arms, N=1000 players, T=200 periods, R=20 trials, K=0.03, DELTA=1.0, U_MAX=2.0, PATIENCE=3.0, D_HIGH=0.3, LAMBDA_CONTROL=1.0, LAMBDA_REAL=2.25, SIGMA_GATE=3.0) three gates are evaluated in order G1 → G2 → G3 off the paired-reduction SEs.
