# VERDICT 143 ‚Äî annual-prepay financing trap

Reproduce PROPOSAL 130 (round-30 VENTURE slot, P130 ‚Üí V143, +13): the annual-prepay financing trap ‚Äî subscription cash-flow economics / venture-domain. The folk model of an annual-prepay discount is that it is free money: "give the customer X% off to pay a year up front and we get 12 months of cash now instead of dribbled out monthly ‚Äî great for cash flow." It is NOT free. Selling a 12-month revenue stream for a discounted lump sum is a LOAN the firm takes FROM its customer, and the discount is the interest. Relative to billing monthly, the firm receives 12¬∑(1‚àíd) at t=0 instead of 1 per month for 12 months ‚Äî it has borrowed ~11 months of revenue and repays by forgoing the discount d¬∑12. The implied financing rate is the internal rate of return of that incremental cash flow: the monthly rate r* solving S(r*)=Œ£_{t=0}^{11}(1+r*)^{‚àít}=12¬∑(1‚àíd) (an annuity-due), with implied annual cost APR_implied=(1+r*)^12‚àí1. Two facts the instinct misses: (1) the implied financing APR is HUGE ‚Äî a modest-looking "2 months free" (d=1/6‚âà16.67%) implies r*‚âà3.5%/mo so APR_implied‚âà51%, i.e. the firm is borrowing ~11 months of its own future revenue at ~51%/yr; (2) at any realistic cost of capital the discounted deal DESTROYS NPV ‚Äî with r_true=1%/mo (‚âà12.68% APR) monthly billing is worth S(0.01)=11.367628 months of present-value revenue vs the annual-prepay deal's 12¬∑(1‚àíd)=10.0, a loss of ~1.37 months (‚âà11.40% of contract value), and the FAIR break-even discount is only d*=1‚àíS(r_true)/12=0.052698 (‚âà5.27%, ~0.63 months) so the market-norm "2 months free" over-discounts by 3.162697√ó. The operator lesson: a prepay discount is not free cash ‚Äî it is implicit borrowing against future revenue whose implied APR is the IRR of the cash-timing swap, so PRICE THE DISCOUNT AS DEBT ‚Äî compute its implied APR, reject it whenever that exceeds your true cost of capital, cap the discount at the fair break-even d*=1‚àíS(r_true)/N, and only exceed d* when genuinely capital-constrained above the implied rate or when quantified lock-in churn savings pay for it; cheap-capital firms should default to periodic billing and never justify a prepay discount by "cash flow" alone.
