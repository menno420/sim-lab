## What this verdict does

Reproduces PROPOSAL 203 (P203 → V216, +13 offset, lane superbot-games): **voting power is
not voting weight.** In a weighted voting game `[q; w_1..w_n]` a coalition `S` wins iff its
total weight `≥ q`; voter `i` is a swing (critical) in `S` iff `S` loses but `S ∪ {i}`
wins. The Banzhaf index normalizes swing counts; the Shapley–Shubik index normalizes
pivotal shares over orderings. Both are computed EXACTLY (`fractions.Fraction`) by
coalition enumeration, with Banzhaf cross-checked by an exact generating-function DP. The
head: **a voter's power is not proportional to its weight** — under `[51; 50,49,1]` the
49-weight bloc has IDENTICAL power to the 1-weight bloc (Banzhaf `1/5` each, Shapley–Shubik
`1/6` each) while the 50-weight bloc holds `3×` the Banzhaf power (`3/5`); positive-weight
DUMMIES (weight yet zero power) occur; and the two canonical indices disagree. Copies the
