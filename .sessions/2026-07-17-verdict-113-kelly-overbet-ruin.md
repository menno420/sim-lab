## 💡 Session idea
The overbet-ruin trap is proved under KNOWN p — but the operational failure mode is p ESTIMATION ERROR, and it is asymmetric in a way that makes full-Kelly fragile even when it is nominally optimal. If p is estimated as p̂ from a finite sample, the plug-in Kelly stake f̂=2p̂−1 inherits the estimation noise, and because g(f) is concave with a hard negative cliff just past f* (here f*=0.20, ruin edge f_c=0.389 — only ~2× f* away, exactly the double-Kelly point), an OVERESTIMATE of p pushes the stake toward the ruin boundary far more destructively than an equal underestimate costs (the downside is unbounded log-loss, the upside is a bounded growth shortfall). That asymmetry is the textbook rationale for FRACTIONAL Kelly (stake λ·f*, λ≈0.5) as robust shrinkage. The natural next fleet-external slice pins an estimation-noise model — draw p̂ ~ Beta from a sample of size n, stake the plug-in Kelly f̂ vs half-Kelly 0.5·f̂ — and pre-registers TWO gates: (1) under p-uncertainty, half-Kelly strictly dominates full-Kelly in MEDIAN (time-average) realized growth at ≥3σ while the ensemble arithmetic mean still favors full-Kelly (the ergodicity split survives estimation noise), and (2) the dominance gap WIDENS as the sample n shrinks (more estimation error → more overbet ruin), verified monotone across an n-ladder. That converts the one-number "bet f*=2p−1" finding into the actionable rule "shrink your Kelly stake in proportion to how little you trust p" — a genuinely different mechanism (estimation-risk shrinkage vs known-p ensemble/time-average split) from this slice, seed-pinnable and ≥3σ-gateable.
