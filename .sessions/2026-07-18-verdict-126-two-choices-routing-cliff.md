## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins a SINGLE-SHOT, FRESH-SIGNAL world — the dispatcher reads every worker's EXACT current load at placement time, and tasks arrive one at a time so probe d always sees the true least-loaded of its sample. That fresh-signal assumption is exactly what makes d=2 double-exponential; it is also the assumption the proposal's own transferable correction ("reinvest the saved coordination budget in load-signal freshness") leans on — yet the current gates never exercise it. The load-bearing generalization the gates do NOT touch is SIGNAL STALENESS: in a real dispatcher the load a probe reads is DELAYED (batched heartbeats, a τ-old cache, or B tasks placed against the same snapshot before it refreshes), and under stale signals the two-choices benefit degrades and can invert into HERD behavior — many dispatchers independently pick the same "least-loaded" worker that looked idle in the stale snapshot and pile onto it, so d=2-on-stale can be WORSE than d=1-on-fresh (Mitzenmacher's "load balancing with stale information" / the thundering-herd regime). A genuinely different next FLEET slice pins the staleness axis the freshness correction implicitly bets on: hold the m=n balls-into-bins frame but place tasks in BATCHES of size B against a snapshot refreshed only every B placements (or with a τ-step delay), and pre-register (G-a) a staleness-degradation gate — the d=2 max load rises monotonically and ≥3σ with the staleness lag B (fresh d=2 ~3.08 → measurably worse as B grows); (G-b) a herd-inversion gate — there exists a staleness B* beyond which d=2-on-stale max load EXCEEDS d=1-on-fresh max load by ≥3σ (the two-choices advantage is destroyed, then reversed, by stale load signals); and (G-c) a freshness-vs-probes budget control — one unit of staleness reduction (halving B) lifts d=2 max load by a ≥3σ larger margin than adding a third probe (d=2→3) does, proving the proposal's "spend the saved budget on load-signal freshness, never on more probes" claim is itself gate-able and directional — converting this slice's fresh-signal "cap fan-out at 2" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "on a real dispatcher the SECOND axis is signal freshness: two fresh probes beat two stale probes beat one fresh probe, and past d=2 every coordination dollar belongs on shrinking the staleness lag, not on more probes." A seed-pinnable stale-load / thundering-herd mechanism distinct from this slice's fresh-signal cliff and from the outbox's single-shot probe-count contrast.
