Reproduce PROPOSAL 151 (round-35 GAME slot, P151 → V164, +13): the St. Petersburg cap-collapse. A "press-your-luck" doubling jackpot — continue with probability p=0.5 (pot ×= r=2), bank on the first tails (prob (1−p)·p^k for k continues, payout r^k) — has divergent uncapped EV (diverges iff p·r ≥ 1), but the house caps the payout at r^M so the realized payout = r^min(k, M). The CAPPED fair price is the closed form EV_cap(p, r, M) = (1−p)·((p·r)^M − 1)/(p·r − 1) + (p·r)^M, which on the critical curve p·r = 1 reduces to (1−p)·M + 1 = M/2 + 1 for fair doubling — FINITE and LOGARITHMIC in the cap (doubling the bankroll M → M+1 adds (1−p)=0.5 coins). The folk belief: "an infinite-expected-value jackpot is priceless — charge anything, or as a player pay anything; a doubling press-your-luck gamble is worth a fortune." It is NOT: the uncapped sample mean never converges (it is dominated by a few rare mega-jackpots and grows without bound as runs are added), so the only stable price is the closed form of the CAPPED game — a handful of coins, and almost entirely INSENSITIVE to the cap (log, not linear). Pricing the jackpot as a fixed fraction of the maximum payout overcharges by orders of magnitude.
